Can machines read our minds? Not exactly. BUT:

1. Is it possible for paralyzed patients to control robotic arms just by thinking of specific actions? Absolutely. https://lnkd.in/eEdR_KD6

2. Can people with speech impairments communicate just by using their brain activity? That's been done too. https://lnkd.in/ewav-tHD

A technology called brain-computer interfaces or BCIs makes this possible. A BCI can translate a user's intention into a real world action. But how do they work? How do scientists go about developing and testing them?

I want to break down this process into accessible bits of info. Apart from channeling my inner Feynman :), I will try to do this by reproducing/following steps of a publication that uses a very common method for designing BCIs. 

So we go from learning about the experiments the authors designed, to methods that were used to interpret brain activity and finally, the conclusions the authors came to. The paper I will follow is titled "Classification of motor imagery EEG using deep learning increases performance in inefficient BCI users" (Tibrewal N, Leeuwis N, Alimardani M (2022)).
